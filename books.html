    If This Is A Man

    Primo Levi  ·  Apr 3, 2026
    A firsthand account of survival in Auschwitz, written by Italian chemist Primo Levi. More than a memoir — it's a precise and unflinching examination of what the camp system did to human identity, told not with rage but with a scientist's clarity that makes it all the more devastating.

    Room

    Emma Donoghue  ·  Jan 9, 2026
    My sister recommended this. A story told entirely from the POV of a five-year-old who has never left the room he was born in — which is actually a shed in someone's backyard. The premise sounds gimmicky, but it works. The ending wrecked me a little.

    A Marriage at Sea

    Sophie Elmhirst  ·  Jan 13, 2026
    A couple decides to sail across the Pacific with almost no experience. Their boat sinks. They spend 118 days on a raft. The survival story is gripping, but what I kept thinking about was how much you learn about a person — and yourself — when there's nowhere to go.

    The Tiger

    John Vaillant  ·  Jan 19, 2026
    A tiger in Siberia decides to hunt a human — methodically, over days. It's terrifying and the setting makes it worse. Vaillant writes the landscape so well you feel cold reading it. If you like narrative nonfiction with genuine stakes, this is a good one.
    NonfictionTrue CrimeAdventure

    Co-Intelligence

    Ethan Mollick  ·  Feb 9, 2026
    Mollick is one of the clearest thinkers writing about AI right now, and there's a lot of noise out there. This is practical and genuinely interesting. I rated it a 6 mostly because I came in with a lot of context — if you're newer to the space, it's probably an 8.
    NonfictionAI & Tech

    The Art Thief

    Michael Finkel  ·  Feb 16, 2026
    The most successful art thief in history wasn't doing it for money — he just genuinely loved the paintings. Stashed a billion dollars worth of art in his apartment. Finkel tells the story in a way that makes you almost root for the guy, which is its own kind of trick. Couldn't put it down.
    NonfictionTrue Crime
    9/10

    Man's Search for Meaning

    Viktor E. Frankl  ·  March 2026
    Viktor Frankl's account of surviving four Nazi concentration camps, and the psychological framework — logotherapy — he developed from the experience. The central argument: meaning, not pleasure or power, is the primary human drive. One of those books that quietly rewires how you think about suffering and choice.
    NonfictionPhilosophy
    10/10
